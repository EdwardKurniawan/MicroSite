About Yuki

Yuki Tanaka was born in Osaka but moved to Kanazawa in 2013 to study traditional textile arts at the Ishikawa Prefectural University of the Arts. She stayed. Twelve years later she lives in a machiya townhouse near the Asanogawa river, writes about Ishikawa Prefecture for Japanese and international travel publications, and leads small-group cultural tours of the city.

Her focus is on what she calls "slow Kanazawa" — the parts of the city that don't appear on highlight reels. The sake breweries operating since the 1600s. The wagashi shops where the same family has made the same sweets for seven generations. The Noh theatre performances in the castle grounds that most visitors never know exist. The relationship between the Kaga Yuzen silk tradition and the particular quality of light on the Sea of Japan coast.

She has written for Japan Times, Monocle Travel Guide, Condé Nast Traveller Japan, and Kinfolk. Her guide to Kanazawa's traditional craft districts was published by PIE International in 2023. She is a licensed cultural guide for Ishikawa Prefecture and a member of the Japan Tourism Agency's Regional Tourism Promotion Board.

The Kanazawa Guide was built to share the kind of knowledge that only comes from living somewhere — the restaurant that moved to a smaller location but got better, the temple walk that nobody mentions in the standard guides, the exact month when the Kenroku-en garden looks the way the Maeda clan intended it to look. Everything here reflects what she actually tells people who visit.
