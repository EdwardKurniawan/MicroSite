About Nishi Chaya

Nishi Chaya (西茶屋街) is the smallest of Kanazawa's three official geisha districts, established in 1820 alongside Higashi Chaya by the Maeda clan. Where Higashi Chaya has multiple streets and a full complement of teahouse museums, Nishi Chaya is essentially one elegant block — a single preserved street of ochaya (teahouses) with latticed facades and a free museum inside the Nishi Chaya Museum (Shima-ke).

Because fewer visitors make the effort to reach it — it requires a bus ride rather than a short walk from the castle area — Nishi Chaya retains an atmosphere closer to what the district would have felt like before tourism. Geisha still use these streets. Occasionally, on a quiet morning, you'll hear shamisen practice drifting through a shoji screen.

The neighbourhood sits in the Teramachi (Temple Town) area, adjacent to over 60 temples that line the left bank of the Sai river. This makes it the natural start or end point for the Teramachi Temple Trail. The Ninja Temple (Myoryuji) is a 5-minute walk away — making a morning that combines Myoryuji, Nishi Chaya, and the Teramachi walk one of the most satisfying in Kanazawa.

When to visit: Early morning (9–10am) is the best time — the light is soft, the street is quiet, and the museum has just opened. By midday on weekends, small tour groups arrive. Avoid visiting after 4pm when the museum closes and the shops begin to shut.

Top Things To Do

  1. 1
    Free admission

    Nishi Chaya Museum (Shima-ke)

    Inside a restored ochaya, this small museum shows the layout and furnishings of a working Edo-period teahouse — the reception rooms where geisha performed, the inner chambers for high-ranking samurai guests, the kitchen and service areas. Small, completely uncrowded, and free. Open daily 9:30am–5pm. It takes about 20–30 minutes; allocate more if you want to understand what you're looking at.

  2. 2
    Shopping

    Gold leaf craft shopping

    Several shops along the main street sell Kanazawa gold leaf products — the city produces 99% of Japan's gold leaf. Look for chopsticks, lacquered boxes, folding fans, and cosmetics incorporating gold leaf. Hakuichi is the flagship store with a workshop viewing area where you can watch the beating process. Higher-end than the tourist shops near Kenroku-en, and genuinely better quality.

  3. 3
    5 min walk

    Combine with Myoryuji (Ninja Temple)

    The Ninja Temple is a 5-minute walk from Nishi Chaya and requires advance booking (reservations fill quickly, especially weekends). If you have a morning reservation at Myoryuji, spend the remainder of the morning in Nishi Chaya. The combination covers two of Kanazawa's most interesting but least-visited attractions in half a day. The 1-hour guided tour at Myoryuji (¥1,200) covers 29 staircases, 23 rooms, hidden tunnels, and escape hatches.

  4. 4
    90 min walk

    Teramachi Temple Trail

    From Nishi Chaya, walk north along the Sai river to encounter 60+ temples in Teramachi — an extraordinary concentration of religious architecture that most visitors never discover. The trail takes 60–90 minutes at a leisurely pace. The temples range from small neighbourhood shrines to substantial compounds; most are free to enter and entirely uncrowded. Best in late afternoon light.

  5. 5
    ¥2,000 · Reservation recommended

    Matcha at Kaikaro

    Kaikaro is one of the few active ochaya in Nishi Chaya that accepts public visitors for matcha and wagashi (traditional sweets). The interior is a remarkable intact Meiji-period teahouse — the kind of space that most visitors to Japan never get inside. Reserve in advance; the experience costs around ¥2,000 and lasts about 30–40 minutes. It is, by some margin, the most atmospheric place to drink matcha in Kanazawa.

Teramachi Temple Trail

The trail begins at the Sai river bridge near Nishi Chaya and winds north through over 60 temples. Allow 60–90 minutes for the full route.

⛩

Suggested Route from Nishi Chaya

Start: Nishi Chaya — Visit the museum, then cross the Sai river bridge heading north into Teramachi.
Myoryuji (Ninja Temple) — If you have a reservation, this is where to start before Nishi Chaya, or visit after.
Teramachi temple cluster — The densest concentration of temples is around Teramachi 2–4 chome. Walk slowly; most gates are open.
Morihachi Wagashi — Kanazawa's oldest wagashi shop, founded 1625, has a Teramachi branch. Buy seasonal sweets to eat as you walk.
End: Korinbo / Katamachi — The trail deposits you naturally toward Katamachi. Good timing for lunch or an early afternoon break.

Where to Eat

Nishi Chaya is not a dining destination — most visitors combine it with a morning in Katamachi or lunch near the Sai river. These are the best options within the neighbourhood itself.

  • Kaikaro
    Matcha and wagashi inside an active ochaya. The most atmospheric eating experience in Nishi Chaya — you're sitting in an intact Meiji-period teahouse. Reservation required. Not a full meal; more an experience.
    ¥¥ · Reserve
  • Teramachi Noodles
    A simple, reliable soba shop near the temple trail — hand-cut buckwheat noodles served cold (zaru soba) or hot in dashi broth. Exactly what you want after an hour of temple walking. Walk-in, no reservations needed.
    ¥
  • Kanazawa Sweets Morihachi
    Kanazawa's oldest wagashi confectionery, founded in 1625. The Teramachi branch is slightly more relaxed than the main store near Kenroku-en. Buy nerikiri (moulded sweet bean paste) to take away, or sit for a set with green tea.
    ¥

Getting There

Transport to Nishi Chaya

  • Loop Bus: Hirokoji stop — 15 minutes from Kanazawa Station. The most convenient option; buses run every 15–20 minutes.
  • On foot from Nagamachi: 15-minute walk south along the Sai river. A pleasant route through the samurai district.
  • On foot from 21st Century Museum: 20-minute walk west along Kenroku-en's southern edge, then south into Teramachi.
  • Best combined with: Myoryuji Ninja Temple (5 min walk) + Teramachi Temple Trail (60–90 min walk north).
Booking Myoryuji: The Ninja Temple requires advance reservation — visit myouryuji.or.jp or ask your hotel to call. Weekend slots in cherry blossom and autumn season book up weeks ahead. Book before you arrive in Kanazawa.
