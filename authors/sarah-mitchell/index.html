About Sarah

I moved to Amsterdam from London in early 2020 — which, in retrospect, was interesting timing. Spending a pandemic lockdown in a city with no tourists turned out to be one of the best ways to actually understand a place. I walked every canal, visited every neighbourhood market, and discovered that Amsterdam is a very different city when it belongs to the people who live in it.

I'd been a travel writer for two years before moving — contributing to print magazines and online guides focused on European city breaks. But Amsterdam changed how I write. When you live somewhere rather than visit it, your perspective on what's worth recommending shifts completely. The standard tourist checklist starts to feel hollow. You learn which "must-see" attractions actually justify the queue and which are overrated. You discover the restaurants locals actually eat at, the market stalls worth the early morning trip, and the museum galleries your fellow visitors walk straight past.

My writing for Amsterdam Insider is grounded in that local knowledge. Every tip, recommendation, and booking warning in my Amsterdam guides is based on firsthand experience — not a press trip itinerary. When I say the Anne Frank House sells out 6–8 weeks ahead in summer, it's because I've watched dozens of tourists turn up without a ticket and be turned away. When I recommend the A'DAM Lookout's free ferry connection, it's because I take that ferry regularly just to feel the city from the water.

I'm also a trained photographer, and all images in my guides that aren't sourced from image libraries were photographed on location. I believe the visual quality of a travel guide is as important as the copy — bad photography makes a beautiful city look generic.
