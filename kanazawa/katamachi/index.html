About Katamachi

Katamachi is the living centre of Kanazawa — not the tourist centre, which is Kenroku-en and the chaya districts, but the place where people actually live, eat, and socialise. It sits between Nagamachi and the Sai river, centred on the Korinbo shopping area and the narrow lanes of the Katamachi Scramble entertainment district.

Unlike the carefully preserved historic districts, Katamachi feels genuinely contemporary — neon-lit yakitori counters, ramen shops with handwritten menus, craft beer bars, and standing seafood restaurants serving the day's catch from Omicho Market a few streets away. The neighbourhood wakes up slowly and stays awake late.

The Korinbo 109 department store marks the main intersection. From there, the best eating and drinking radiates through the side streets to the south and west. The Kinki Izakaya strip along Katamachi 2-chome is where you'll find the densest concentration of drinking places.

Local tip: Katamachi is a neighbourhood that rewards wandering without a plan. Pick a direction from the Korinbo intersection, follow the sound of conversation, and trust the lit doorways. The best izakayas in Kanazawa have no websites.

Top Things To Do

  1. 1

    Evening izakaya crawl

    The best approach to Katamachi is to pick a direction from Korinbo and walk, looking for lit doorways and the sound of voices. Most izakayas seat 8–20 people; the best ones have handwritten seasonal menus on the wall. Order whatever has been freshly written — it means the kitchen is proud of it today. Budget ¥3,000–4,500 per person for drinks and food.

  2. 2

    Omicho Market morning

    Katamachi is a 10-minute walk from Omicho Market, Kanazawa's 300-year-old covered seafood labyrinth. Go early — before 9:30am — for the freshest seafood breakfast bowl (kaisen-don) before visiting the rest of the neighbourhood. The market stalls are most atmospheric on weekday mornings when the restaurants are buying.

  3. 3

    Korinbo shopping

    The Korinbo and Tatemachi covered arcades offer a mix of craft shops — Kanazawa gold leaf goods, lacquerware, traditional textiles — alongside everyday Japanese retail. The D&Department Ishikawa inside the 21st Century Museum (a short walk away) has the best-curated regional crafts selection in the city, if you want a single place to find serious Kanazawa-made goods.

  4. 4

    Craft beer

    Kanazawa has a small but quality craft beer scene centred on Katamachi. Gold Leaf Brewing is the local standout — their IPA has actual edible gold leaf floating in it, which is either gimmick or genius depending on your temperament. Jazzbar Fukurou (jazz and whisky, no craft beer but worth knowing) is a nearby institution.

  5. 5

    Late-night ramen

    Several excellent ramen shops stay open until 2–3am for post-drinking recovery — a service this neighbourhood has always understood. Mugen serves a rich, lingering tonkotsu; Menya Taiga is the local recommendation for spicy Kaga miso ramen, which uses regional miso and has a depth of flavour you won't find in Tokyo chains. Expect a short queue after midnight on weekends.

Where to Eat & Drink

Six of the best places to eat and drink in Katamachi — from counter sushi to late-night ramen. All genuinely recommended by locals.

Sushi Counter
Omicho Ichiba-sushi
Counter sushi using fish sourced directly from Omicho market each morning. The omakase at lunch is exceptional value — the chef selects whatever arrived freshest. Reserve for dinner; lunch is walk-in.

Yoshoku (Japanese-Western)
Grill Otsuka
Western-style yoshoku cooking since 1951. The omurice (omelette over rice with demi-glace sauce) and hayashi rice are the things to order. A warm, slightly worn room that feels like the city's memory. ¥¥
¥¥
Ramen
Ippudo Kanazawa
The Kanazawa outpost of the reliable Fukuoka tonkotsu chain. Not the most local option, but consistently excellent for a post-evening bowl when the specialist ramen shops have queues. Open late.
¥¥
Yakitori
Yakitori Kaichi
Charcoal yakitori served at a tiny 8-seat counter behind a noren curtain. No menu in English, but the counter staff will point you toward what's good tonight. Go for the tsukune (minced chicken) and the negima.
¥¥
Sushi Counter
Kanazawa Manten Sushi
One of the best-value sushi counters in the city, with the Noto Peninsula and Sea of Japan catch running through everything on the menu. The fatty yellowtail (buri) and snow crab in season are not to be missed.
¥¥¥
Whisky Bar
Bar Keller
A long-standing basement whisky bar with an impressive Suntory and Nikka selection, plus older single malts and some good independent bottlings. Serious but not pretentious. Perfect for 10pm onwards.

Getting There

Transport Options

  • Loop Bus: Korinbo stop (all lines pass through, 20 minutes from Kanazawa Station). Most convenient option — the bus runs every 15 minutes during the day.
  • On foot from Nagamachi: 15-minute walk east through the samurai district. Best way to arrive if you've spent the afternoon at Kenroku-en.
  • On foot from Higashi Chaya: 20-minute walk west across the Asanogawa river. A pleasant late-afternoon stroll.
  • Taxi from station: ¥600–800, 8–10 minutes. Worth it after dark or with luggage.
